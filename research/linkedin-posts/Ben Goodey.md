We're seeing a lot of big sites getting hammered lately. Losing +90% of their traffic within a month. 

It happened to one of our clients. Years of hard work...very quickly gone.

Here's a few ways to protect yourself:

1. Conduct a thorough website security review.

Wordpress is how our client got hacked (turned into a fake casino), and I'm sure it's the source of many hacks across the internet. Their plugin ecosystem is extremely vulnerable. Remove all unnecessary plugins immediately and get somebody who knows what they're doing to thoroughly review and improve your security.

2. Stay away from AI-generated programmatic SEO projects.

Short term, it may seem fun and exciting to capture tons of traffic while underinvesting in the quality of the pages doing so. But long term, when it destroys your website's credibility, helpful user signals, and gets you on the wrong side of Google's spam detectors, your short-term desires for growth will ruin the long-term, sustainable growth that SEO can and should be bringing you. 

3. Invest in content quality

So tempting right now to use AI content. It's also been the prevailing wisdom of bad SEO to regurgitate consensus content on the Internet as your content strategy. But right now, you should be doing everything you can to avoid commodity content, bringing new insight with genuine expertise to the table. Make things that real people will like to read. 

4. Always beware the latest best practices and trends

They are unproven over the long term. The latest is the low-quality bottom-of-funnel listicle for AEO. As a company who's expertise area is bottom-up funnel SEO, I'm definitely not against creating high-quality, well-researched, compliant bottom-of-funnel content. But spamming them out purely to influence AI search and not serve your customer, and without editorial guidelines or full funnel SEO, is a no-no. 

5. Invest in brand building

The sites we see being protected and performing the best long-term are those with the biggest, most trusted brands. All SEO practices should be done within the wider scope of proven best practices. Brand building is one of those. It improves your on-page user experience signals, your click-through rate, and ultimately, if things do go tits up, you'll still get your brand traffic to insulate you.

Godspeed 🫡

There's no way AI/agents can replace a good SEO.

At high levels, it's too strategic and nuanced a role. And there's too much misinformation, experimentation, and innovation required.

But can it make a good SEO even better?

I love the idea of AI as an enabler in SEO.

I've started building my own micro-tools using Lovable & Claude that are 100% custom to my own workflows. 

In the past 2 weeks, I built:

- tool that uses Search Console API to find trends that I don't otherwise have time to look into on a daily basis.

- an automated reporting tool (GA4 API) that tracks our client's highest priority URLs and pings Slack if conversions or sessions drop suddenly.

- a tool that's accelerating AI citation outreach for our clients.

And we're using Claude Projects to ensure our content is compliant and on-tone for each client.

These genuinely enable our SEO and content teams to do more, better work for clients.

They also kill a % of the manual & repetitive work.

If I joined a startup as their first marketer tomorrow, here’s the SEO/AI SEO content strategy I’d build to drive growth (and futureproof it):

This strategy covers:

+ Clicks
+ AI Visibility
+ Brand Building

𝐘𝐨𝐮𝐫 𝐁𝐫𝐚𝐧𝐝 & 𝐏𝐫𝐨𝐝𝐮𝐜𝐭 𝐁𝐚𝐬𝐞 𝐋𝐚𝐲𝐞𝐫

This layer is essential for capturing website visitors, as well as coaching platforms like ChatGPT & Perplexity why they should recommend you.

∙ Create a positioning and messaging doc with key differentiators.
∙ Create one landing page for every category, use case and persona. 
∙ Create a "brand explainer" help center.
∙ Specifically address key attributes in detail ("What makes X so accurate?").
∙ Setup platforms like G2 and start collecting reviews ASAP.
∙ Setup YouTube and create 'foundational' content demo'ing the product.

𝐘𝐨𝐮𝐫 𝐇𝐢𝐠𝐡 𝐈𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐧𝐭 𝐂𝐨𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐧𝐭 𝐏𝐫𝐨𝐝𝐮𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧/𝐎𝐩𝐬 𝐋𝐚𝐲𝐞𝐫

This layer will set you up for consistent content operations AND create the first layer of high intent content that drives conversions & recommendations.

∙ Find 2-4 high quality freelancer writers (don't take shortcuts here!).
∙ Setup your project management (Notion, Slack, GDrive, etc)
∙ Map all high intent keyword opportunities.
∙ Give your writers the landing pages & positioning doc you made 🏆
∙ Build out all X vs Y, Best X, and X Reviews pattern content FIRST.
∙ Use templates to shortcut your outlines^.
∙ Create a downloadable buyers guide to capture more leads.

𝐘𝐨𝐮𝐫 𝐎𝐟𝐟-𝐒𝐢𝐭𝐞 𝐒𝐭𝐫𝐚𝐭𝐞𝐠𝐲 𝐋𝐚𝐲𝐞𝐫

This layer will build up trust and awareness of your brand across the internet...which is essential for ranking in both traditional and AI search.

∙ Start backlink building immediately. It's a life force.
∙ Run 2-4 digital PR campaigns per year to get foundations in place.
∙ List 20 newsletters in your industry - sponsor them (awareness + link).
∙ Build partnerships through your investors and write guest posts.
∙ Answer 1,000s of Featured and HARO requests (yes it is back!)
∙ Reddit: Encourage your raving fans to show up on the right threads.
∙ Youtube: Get influencers to review & recommend your product.

𝐘𝐨𝐮𝐫 𝐀𝐰𝐚𝐫𝐞𝐧𝐞𝐬𝐬 & 𝐓𝐫𝐮𝐬𝐭 𝐋𝐚𝐲𝐞𝐫

This layer is because bottom of funnel works 10x better when supported with a solid top of funnel content strategy. Great SEO isn't done in isolation of brand building.

∙ Do YouTube shorts & explainers for every topic in your niche.
∙ These now show up everywhere in the SERPs and AI Overviews.
∙ Build them into (carefully) chosen pain point articles
∙ Build a content flywheel: 
∙ Founder podcast → social content → build community

Yep - this is a looooot of work.

The whole company needs to be behind you to pull it off well.

But work fast and roll it out in stages...and within a year, you'll have built a compounding growth engine, driven by search and an AI recommendation engines.

(I've really used all this to build multiple clients to +$1m ARR in 12 months)
