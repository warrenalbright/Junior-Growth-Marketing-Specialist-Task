## Article 1: When Scrutiny Becomes Cheap
- **URL:** https://www.jonoalderson.com/conjecture/when-scrutiny-becomes-cheap/
- **Date Published:** June 25, 2026
- **Type:** Blog Article
- **Author:** Jono Alderson

### Summary
Published the same day this research was collected, Jono argues that AI
has collapsed the cost of scrutiny — meaning organizations that were
previously "good enough" because inspection was expensive will now be
exposed. He uses the Anthropic Mythos security incident as a jumping-off
point to argue that this same economic shift is coming for content,
messaging, and brand claims. Any organization optimized for the level of
scrutiny they expected to receive, rather than for genuinely being good,
is now at risk. Directly relevant to AI-powered SEO content production:
the bar for content quality, accuracy, and brand consistency has been
permanently raised by AI systems that can evaluate everything cheaply
and at scale.

## Article 2: The Web Isn't URL-Shaped Anymore
- **URL:** https://www.jonoalderson.com/conjecture/url-shaped-web/
- **Date Published:** July 30, 2025
- **Type:** Blog Article
- **Author:** Jono Alderson

### Summary
Jono argues that decades of SEO have been built around the URL as the
fundamental unit of optimization — but AI systems have already moved
past this model. They don't care about the envelope (the URL); they
care about the assertions inside it and how those assertions connect to
everything else they know. He outlines a shift from optimizing documents
to optimizing the clarity and connectivity of brand claims — using
semantic HTML, machine-friendly endpoints, third-party validation, and
a coherent public record that AI systems can trust and extract meaning
from. One of the clearest frameworks available for understanding what
AI-powered SEO content production must become.

## Article 3: Technical Debt is Becoming an AI Problem
- **URL:** https://www.jonoalderson.com/conjecture/technical-debt/
- **Date Published:** June 22, 2026
- **Type:** Blog Article
- **Author:** Jono Alderson

### Summary
Jono examines how the current wave of AI standards — llms.txt, WebMCP,
Universal Commerce Protocol — is revealing an uncomfortable truth about
how businesses handle change. The traditional approach of waiting for
standards to consolidate before committing resources no longer works
when meaningful shifts happen every few weeks rather than every few
years. Technical debt accumulated over two decades of web development is
now becoming an AI visibility problem — businesses that delay adapting
to AI protocols risk being invisible to the systems that increasingly
mediate discovery. Directly relevant to anyone building an AI-powered
SEO content production strategy that needs to account for technical
infrastructure, not just content.
